The VIP Problem
VIP revenue collapsed 94% (₹9.8L → ₹57K/month). They returned at the same rate as everyone else (12%) despite being the most loyal pre-crisis. This confirms the crisis was severe enough to erase even the strongest loyalty signal — they need active outreach, not passive win-back.

Strategy
3,823 VIPs are lost. Each is worth ₹226/month. This is the single highest-ROI customer recovery action available.

Recovery Recommendations — Prioritised by ImpactAction plan
🔴 Urgent — Fix Now
Delivery Infrastructure: Route Optimisation
Avg delivery time is 60 min (SLA: 42.5 min). 87.7% of orders breach SLA. This is the #1 driver of cancellations (12%) and poor ratings (2.47★). Implement real-time route optimisation, peak-hour driver surge pricing, and 20-min delivery zones for high-density areas.
Target: ≤45 min avg · SLA breach ≤65% · Cancel rate ≤8%
🔴 Urgent — Fix Now
Fix is_active Data Quality in dim_restaurant
4,867 churned restaurants are still marked is_active = "Y". This inflates active partner counts used for investor reporting and ops planning. Run a weekly batch job: mark is_active = "N" for any restaurant with zero orders in trailing 30 days.
Impact: Accurate KPI reporting · Prevents false ops confidence
🟡 High Priority
Champion Win-Back Campaign
24,783 Champion-segment customers (highest LTV, avg ₹590 spend) returned at only 11.9%. Since all segments returned equally, loyalty alone won't bring them back — they need active outreach. Personalised email + ₹150 credit + food safety certification proof. Target: 25% return rate.
If 25% return: +6,195 high-value customers · ~₹3.7L incremental revenue
🟡 High Priority
Restaurant Re-onboarding: North Indian & Biryani First
North Indian (1,060 churned) and Biryani (759 churned) are the highest-volume cuisine segments lost. These cuisines drive the most repeat orders nationally. Offer 0% commission for 60 days + safety certification support. Prioritise verified cloud kitchens.
Target: Recover 40% of churned North Indian restaurants in Q4
🔵 Medium Priority
Shift Acquisition: Paid → Referral
45.1% of recovery users came via Paid ads — highest CAC channel with uncertain retention. Referral users have inherently higher trust (social proof post-crisis). Launch "Bring Back a Friend" — ₹100 credit for both referrer and referee. Paid currently dominates at 45.1% (high CAC) — shifting even 10% budget to referral reduces acquisition cost significantly.
Referral CAC typically 3–5× lower than Paid · Higher 90-day retention
🔵 Medium Priority
Address Declining Recovery Sentiment
Rating fell from 2.63 (crisis) to 2.31 (Sep recovery) — getting worse. Customers who stayed are most frustrated. Immediate: proactive SMS/push after every delayed order with compensation credit. Mid-term: real-time customer satisfaction monitoring dashboard for ops team.
Target: Recovery avg rating ≥3.5★ by Dec 2025
🟢 Long-Term
Build Crisis-Resilient Operations
The June monsoon outage + food safety incident created a cascading failure that wiped 88% of the customer base. Build: (1) geographically distributed delivery partner pools to avoid single-region outages, (2) food safety audit scores published in-app, (3) automated SLA monitoring with real-time alerts.
Moat: Trust infrastructure as competitive differentiator vs Swiggy/Zomato
🟢 Long-Term
Recovery North Star Metric
Current recovery is volume-flat (Jul–Sep ~8,600 orders/month). Define a single North Star: Monthly Active Customers ≥30,000 by Mar 2026. Track weekly. Gate all initiatives against this metric. Decompose into: (1) win-back rate, (2) new user retention, (3) order frequency.
